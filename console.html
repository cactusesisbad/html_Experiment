>Beingin

during march 24-28 whilst attending a gamedev course course arranged by my school i had started to day dream. prior to this i did have some gamedev experience and even made a flappy bird clone so i had breezed throught the entire course on day one whilst my peers where learning to write in c#. i was discussing with my teacher and brought up the idea of making a game without any engine and that idea drilled itself into my head like a worm.

>messing about

i had searched up and found out that the current language i was using could run on the CLI and that there were online intereperts that could do such things online. for the next year or so i used my PLD to develope parts of a game and stiched it up into a buggy mess.i had learnt a lot of things such as not taking auto complete for granted saving your work before leaving and patients.

>problems

just as i was going to do a refactoring of my code to fix long standing bugs the mobile guardian attacks happened and i lost all my work only old prototypes remained. i was genuine more dishearten to find all my time and effort on the project gone to waste then all the school photos i had on it. i quit trying to do anything coding or computing from the heartbreak and burnout

>second times the charm right?

3mounths later when i was using my computer i releasised i was being unproductive and that i had not been challenged in quite some time so reluctantly i started to redo the entire project from stratch. as i already ruffly knew how to draw a "display" and handle "player collision" i estimated it to take 3 weeks.

>how fast?

i finished it 8hours.i didnt see the time fly by but from 12 pm to 8 pm i was working on it no stop and the extra functionsality of VS supercharged developemnt

>take away

from this project my main take aways were use morden tools do waste time for nothing build tools if you need them , also its fun! save your data using the 321 rule design first then code or else you end up with a undercooked idea refactor it when its too hard to understand your own code COMMENT COMMENT COMMENT you are not the 0.000001% of people who are able to remember everything perfectly
